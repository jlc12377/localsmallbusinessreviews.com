About Miami's Financial Services Sector

Why Miami financial advisors matter and what to look for.

Selecting a financial advisor in Miami requires careful evaluation. The city is home to a wide range of advisors — from independent RIA fiduciaries to multi-family offices to wirehouse representatives. The differences in service models, fee structures, and client interests can be dramatic.

The right financial advisor understands Miami's context — wealth concentration, real estate-heavy portfolios, international tax considerations, business succession planning, and the planning needs of high-net-worth households.

Our rankings prioritize advisors with verified credentials (CFP, CFA, fiduciary status), Miami client histories, transparent fee structures, and recognized industry standing.

Our Ranking Methodology

AUM & Industry Recognition (35%)

Verified assets under management plus rankings from Barron's, Newsweek, Forbes, SmartAsset, and Dakota.

Fiduciary Standards (25%)

Fee-only or fee-based fiduciary status, transparent fee structures, and conflict-of-interest management.

Verified Credentials (20%)

CFP, CFA, RIA registration, and other verified professional qualifications across the team.

Miami Client History (20%)

Established Miami presence, demonstrated client tenure, and Miami market specialization.
